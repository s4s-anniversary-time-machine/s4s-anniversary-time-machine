A question that isn't so obvious but worth asking is where did the pommel come from? The gladius is just the hilt and blade. The longsword however, has a thin pommel. Pommels balance the sword, which is something gladius did not need because of its length. However, it also made something more important easier. When flipped upside down, the classic European longsword becomes a cross. It was used as a quick, pious burial for a fallen crusader on the road. Function does indeed meet form.
